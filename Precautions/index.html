Prepare for evacuation: If you are in a low-lying or flood-prone area, be prepared to evacuate if necessary. Gather important documents, medications, and other necessities and store them in a waterproof and easily accessible location.
Guidelines to Adhere to during a Flood
Turn off utilities: If you are leaving your home, turn off the electricity, gas, and water to reduce the risk of fire or explosion.
Avoid driving: Do not attempt to drive through flooded roads, as even shallow water can be dangerous. If you must drive, take an alternate route and use extreme caution.
Stay informed: Keep up to date on the latest weather and flood conditions, as well as any evacuation orders or alerts issued by local authorities.
Keep in touch: If you are evacuated, let friends and family know your whereabouts and status.
Stay patient: Floods can be stressful, but it's important to remain calm and patient during the recovery process.
